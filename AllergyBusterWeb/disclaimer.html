Medical Disclaimer

Critical information about the scope and limitations of AllergyBuster.

Last updated: April 8, 2026

Emergency: If you or someone else is experiencing a severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) — including difficulty breathing, swelling of the throat, rapid heartbeat, or loss of consciousness — call 911 or your local emergency number immediately. Do not rely on this app during a medical emergency.

Not Medical Advice

AllergyBuster is an informational reference tool. The information provided through this application and website does not constitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. All content is provided for general informational purposes only.

AllergyBuster is not a licensed medical provider, dietitian, or allergist. The app should never be used as a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment from a qualified healthcare provider who knows your complete medical history.

Always Consult a Healthcare Professional

If you have or suspect you have a food allergy, food intolerance, or any related medical condition, you should:

  • Consult a board-certified allergist or physician for proper testing and diagnosis
  • Work with your healthcare provider to develop a personalized allergy management plan
  • Carry any prescribed medications (such as epinephrine auto-injectors) as directed by your doctor
  • Not make changes to your diet or medication based solely on information from this app
  • Regularly review your allergy management plan with your healthcare provider

Data Accuracy and Limitations

AllergyBuster relies on data from third-party sources including Open Food Facts (an open-source database) and community contributors. This data has significant limitations:

  • Formulations change. Manufacturers regularly change product ingredients without immediately updating public databases. A product that was safe last month may contain a new allergen today.
  • Database coverage is incomplete. Not all products are in our database. A "no results found" response does not mean a product is allergen-free.
  • Cross-contamination is not fully captured. "May contain" and shared-equipment warnings may not always be reflected in the data.
  • Restaurant data is community-sourced. Restaurant menus and recipes change frequently. Community-submitted data may be outdated, incomplete, or inaccurate.
  • AI-generated tips are not clinical guidance. Daily allergy tips are generated by artificial intelligence for general interest only. They are not tailored to your individual medical situation.

Always read the physical product label yourself before consuming any food, regardless of what this app shows. The label on the package is the definitive source of allergen information for that product at the time of purchase.

Severity of Allergic Reactions

Food allergies can range from mild discomfort to life-threatening anaphylaxis. Even trace amounts of certain allergens can trigger severe reactions in some individuals. AllergyBuster cannot assess your personal sensitivity level or predict how your body will react to any given product.

If you have a history of severe allergic reactions, exercise extreme caution and consult your allergist before trying any new food product, regardless of what any app or database shows.
